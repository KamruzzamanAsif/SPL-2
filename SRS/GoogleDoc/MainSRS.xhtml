Introduction

Autism, or autism spectrum disorder (ASD), is a complex condition that affects social skills, behavior, communication, and daily tasks. Children with autism often learn differently from other children, and it is important to consider their unique characteristics when developing learning tools for them. In recent years, technology such as computers and smartphones has been found to be engaging for autistic children.

Many resources and applications have been developed to support the learning of autistic children, but they often do not take into account the cultural and local knowledge of children in countries like Bangladesh. For instance, a software module that teaches about different types of fruit may include apples and strawberries for children in first world countries, but for autistic children in Bangladesh, it would be more relevant to include mangoes and jackfruits.

It is important to continue researching and developing resources and tools that are suitable for autistic children in a variety of cultural and local contexts. By doing so, we can ensure that all children with autism have the opportunity to learn and grow to their full potential.

2.2 Usage Scenario of SchoolSmart

The term "autism" or "autism spectrum disorder" (ASD) refers to a wide variety of developmental problems that affect behavior, speech, and social interaction. The development of intelligent devices and applications in technology has produced a number of practical methods for dealing with this sickness quickly and effectively. Even though everyone should have a basic education, teaching the autistic community in a traditional way is still rather difficult. The interaction challenges - such as how autistic children may interact with the learning software or devices - affect ICT-based learning software that has been created for autistic children.

EmPower  is a user-friendly interactive learning tool to provide autistic children with basic academics. To teach the students, EmPower provides different lessons on nouns, verbs, activities, and associations that are made concerning different types of disabilities that necessitate different requirements. There is an interactive Quiz based evaluation system and a system to monitor and track the learning progress of students based on their gaze tracking system.

Students will be taught four kinds of things through the lessons of our learning tool. These are as follows:

  • Nouns i.e. identifying objects - teaching nouns through images, texts, and audio.
  • Verbs i.e. identifying tasks - verbs are the same as nouns i.e. teaching with images, texts, and audio.
  • Activity Scheduling i.e. learning to do work sequentially - Activity scheduling is taught with videos and texts.
  • Association i.e. understanding associations between correlated things - Association may contain either image, texts, and audio or videos and texts.

The instructors can make customized lessons according to specific students' needs. There is a reward system as Autistic children are highly motivated by rewards. Reward refers to a clapping image, a happy emoji or a cartoon video. The instructor can choose a reward according to the student's taste and the student will be rewarded for successfully completing a task.

To evaluate the learning outcomes, there is a Quiz based evaluation system. The evaluation system consists of three options. These are:

  • Matching - There will be an image and a question regarding the image and answer options.
  • Drag & Drop - This is an image drag & drop matching problem. Users have to drag images and drop them in the next section.
  • Jigsaw Puzzle - An image will be divided into multiple parts and these will be shuffled. Users have to match the image parts by drag & drop. There are different levels of difficulty.

While learning the lessons the activities of the students are monitored through the webcam. The software tracks the student’s eyeball (iris) movements, denoting how attentive he/she was while solving quizzes or learning the lessons and all the details will be stored for further analysis. The software provides a statistical analysis based on the collected data and predicts the students' performance.

EmPower is a desktop application. It comes with two modes of usage based on users - Basic mode for students and Advanced mode for instructors. The instructors have to install its Advanced mode on their personal computers and students have to install the Basic mode on their personal computers.

With limited access to all features of our software, students with basic mode can view the lessons, and participate in quizzes. On the contrary, instructors have access to all the features of our software including adding lessons, customizing lessons, creating quizzes, tracking, monitoring individual and cumulative students' performance, and analyzing and viewing the overall summary of the student’s performance in the form of different graphical representations.

Students’ lessons and task data (lessons, quiz performance, and activities) are saved automatically on their system through our software. The guardians of the students collect the data into a storage device (like a USB flash drive) and provide them to instructors. The instructor then copies the data into the system and views the details of the student’s performance of given lessons and quizzes.

The software provides a profile-based monitoring system for the students to track their learning progress individually. All the data from Quiz evaluation and lesson learning are stored for individual students. Instructors can access the data, view their performance, and take measures based on their performance data. The software provides several graphical visualizations such as - pie charts, bar charts, and line graphs to compare the learning curves of students easily.

3 Scenario Based Modeling

The success of a computer-based system or product is measured in many ways but user satisfaction resides at the top of the list. Understanding how actors want to interact with a system will lead the software team to properly characterize requirements and build meaningful analysis and design models. Hence, requirements modeling begins with the creation of scenarios in the form of use cases, activity diagrams, and swimlane diagrams.

3.1 What is a Use Case Diagram?

A use case is a written description of how users will perform tasks on your website. It outlines, from a user's point of view, a system's behavior as it responds to a request. Each use case is represented as a sequence of simple steps, beginning with a user's goal and ending when that goal is fulfilled. Use cases specify the expected behavior (what), and not the exact method of making it happen (how). Use cases once specified can be denoted both textual and visual representation (i.e. use case diagram). A key concept of use case modeling is that it helps us design a system from the end user's perspective. It is an effective technique for communicating system behavior in the user's terms by specifying all externally visible system behavior.

3.2 The Purpose of Use Case Diagram

The reasons why an organization would want to use case diagrams include:

  • Represent the goals of systems and users.
  • Specify the context a system should be viewed in.
  • Specify system requirements.
  • Provide a model for the flow of events when it comes to user interactions.
  • Provide an outside view of a system.
  • Shows external and internal influences on a system.
